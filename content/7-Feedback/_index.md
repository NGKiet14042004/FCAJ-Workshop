### Overall Evaluation

**1. Program Quality**

The program is organized clearly, progressing from basic knowledge to more advanced topics. Each week has specific goals, so I did not feel overwhelmed when first getting used to the cloud environment. Combining theory with hands-on exercises helped me understand the material better, especially the weeks on data storage and databases, which gave me more confidence when starting my assigned work on the team project.

**2. Documentation and Learning Resources**

The program materials and project guides are easy to read, with illustrations and clear task assignments for each member. That helped me know exactly what I needed to do and avoid duplicating or taking on someone else's work. However, some content related to artificial intelligence and storing data for AI systems is still quite new, so I sometimes had to read additional English documentation to understand it more thoroughly.

**3. Relevance Between Program and Project**

What I learned over 12 weeks connected closely to the real project. Topics like networking, file storage, database management, and securing login information all came in handy when deploying ZeroBug Agent. I also gained a better understanding of why the team split work among members instead of having one person handle everything.

**4. Learning and Skill Development Opportunities**

After 12 weeks, I can see clear progress compared to when I started. I worked on data storage, setting up the database, and preparing the foundation for the app's AI features. What impressed me most was building a real product with the team—from the initial idea to putting all the pieces together.

**5. Biggest Challenges and Lessons Learned**

- **Hardest part**: Setting up the database so it was both secure and usable for other team members to connect to later, without exposing passwords in the source code.
- **Also not easy**: Working as a team on a shared cloud account—handing off the right information at the right time so the next person could continue without getting stuck.
- **Important lesson**: Do not write passwords or sensitive information directly into code. Assign roles clearly, communicate often, and double-check before moving to the next step.

**6. Policies and Benefits**

The $200 credit package let me practice with less worry about costs. The program also reminded us early about tracking spending, so I got into the habit of checking costs and shutting down or removing unused resources after finishing each assignment.

### Additional Questions

- What did you find **most satisfying** about participating in the program?

  → Working in a real environment, completing my part of the team project, and having a product I can add to my portfolio.

- What do you think the program **should improve**?

  → More live Q&A sessions or mentor support when teams run into difficult problems during project deployment.

- If recommending to a friend, would you **suggest they join**? Why?

  → Yes. This program suits students who want learning paired with hands-on practice—getting familiar with cloud technology and AI through a well-organized team project.

---

### Suggestions and Expectations

- I hope the program adds more guidance on managing sensitive information (passwords, access keys, etc.) securely, since many students struggle with this when first deploying projects.
- I hope to see more real-world examples of combining databases with AI features in small to medium projects, not just theory.
- Continue the team project model with clear role assignments and expand it so more students can participate.
